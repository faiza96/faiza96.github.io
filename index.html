About Germany

Germany is a Western European country with a landscape of forests, rivers, mountain ranges and North Sea beaches. It has over 2 millennia of history. Berlin, its capital, is home to art and nightlife scenes, the Brandenburg Gate and many sites relating to WW2. Munich is known for its Oktoberfest and beer halls, including the 16th century Hofbrauhaus. Frankfurt, with its skyscrpaers, houses the European Central Bank. Germany has a population of over 80 million people, according to the World Bank.

As you can see from the map, Germany consists of 16 provinces, North Rhine-Westphalia: Nordrhein-Westfalen being the province with the highest population with over 18 million people. Second comes Bavaria: Bayern with over 12 million people and the third most populated is Baden-Württemberg with around 12 million people. Germany is also the second most populated country in the world after Russia, with over 80 million inhabitants.

Germany's history

    Germany before 1871: In the middle of the 19th century, Germany was not a unified country but a collection of over 350 principalities and over 1000 small states. This fragmentation and the feudal nature of the society led to frequent conflict and high levels of poverty.
